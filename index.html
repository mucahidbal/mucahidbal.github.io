Bram Cohen

Early Life

Bram Cohen (Figure 1) was born in 12 September 1975 at America. Cohen grew up in Manhattan and Cohen's father is computer scientist[1], his mother is a teacher[2]. He learned BASIC programming language while he was five years old. He completed the Stuyvesant high school in 1993[3] and he started to the State University of Newyork. Later, he leave the University to work for some dot-com companies. The last company he worked is MojoNation.

BitTorrent

Bram Cohen started to work on BitTorrent(Figure 2) in 2001. In 2002 he organized the CodeCon. CodeCon is an annual organization of hackers and technology fans. From 2002 to 2009, CodeCon is organized every year[4]. Cohen, presented his ideas about BitTorrent at first CodeCon in 2002.

Cohen wrote his first BitTorrent implementation in Python programming language. BitTorrent uses Cohen's own peer-to-peer protocol. BitTorrent becomes famous from its ability to download big video and audio files in short time.

His Awards and Titles

  • 2004 Wired Rave Award
  • 2006 USENIX STUG Award
  • 2010 Internet Evolution 100

Bram Cohen, shown in the "100 Most Influential People" from Time Magazine and MIT showed him in the "25 Most Innovative People Under the Age 35"[1].
